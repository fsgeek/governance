## 1. What was run

For each Cat-2-(pricing) burst, for each sub-grade flagged in the burst's first quarter V₁: build the ε-band of policy-admissible *refinements* on V₁'s grade-G loans — shallow CARTs (`max_depth ∈ {1,2,3}`, `min_samples_leaf ∈ {25,50,100}`) over every non-empty subset of the named features `{fico_range_low, dti, annual_inc, emp_length}`, with the thin-demo policy's monotonicity directions enforced, **sign-flipped to the default-prediction convention** (the encoder states directions w.r.t. grant probability; `dti: negative` w.r.t. grant ⇒ `+1` w.r.t. default; `fico_range_low: positive` w.r.t. grant ⇒ `−1` w.r.t. default; `annual_inc`, `emp_length` unconstrained). Score by holdout AUC for default on a deterministic 70/30 within-grade split; band R(ε) = combos within ε = 0.02 of the best holdout AUC; de-duplicate combos that fit the identical tree. Then measure, per grade: (1) **non-trivial** = ≥ 3 distinct band members; (2) **plural** = ≥ 2 distinct used-feature sets AND median pairwise ranking-Spearman among members < 0.9; (3) **forward-predictive** = a majority of (full-V₁-refit) members beat the constant on V₂'s within-grade default (grade-size-aware label-shuffle null, 95th pct, 500 perms; HIT iff OOS AUC > null and ≥ 0.52); (4) **constraint cheap** = constrained band's best forward-predictive OOS AUC ≥ 0.9 × the unconstrained band's. Baselines: an unconstrained ε-band over the same named features; a single kitchen-sink depth-3 tree over named + 9 extension features. Bursts: **D** (build 2015Q3 → eval 2015Q4, `dti`-dominated), **A** (build 2013Q3 → eval 2013Q4, `annual_inc`-dominated).

## 3. Verdict against the pre-registration

**Mixed — and my pre-registered prediction was wrong on which burst would carry the plurality.**

- **Burst D (2015Q3 → 2015Q4, the load-bearing `dti` burst): claim HOLDS.** Non-trivial on all 7 grades, forward-predictive on 6 (C5 — n≈3k — is the lone miss: all 48 band members clip the null), plural on 5, constraint-cheap on all 7. The plurality is *real*: grade B1's band members rank the tier's borrowers with a median pairwise Spearman of **0.167** — policy-admissible refinements of B1 strongly disagree on who is higher-risk. A5 (0.63), C5 (0.56), D4 (0.63), C1 (0.74) are also genuinely plural. Only A1 (0.96) and C2 (0.95) are "non-trivial but not plural" — DTI-dominated, members near-identical. **My prediction** ("plurality the soft spot on 2015H2; band DTI-heavy, median Spearman ~0.85–0.95") **was too pessimistic** — the 2015H2 bands are more plural than I expected.
- **Burst A (2013Q3 → 2013Q4, the `annual_inc` burst): claim FALSIFIED — specifically the plurality leg.** Non-trivial 5/5, forward-predictive 5/5, constraint-cheap 5/5 — but plural only 2/5 (B2 0.94, B3 0.91, B4 0.95 all collapse to near-agreement). **My prediction** ("similar to D, income-heavy band, plural on a majority") **was wrong** — the `annual_inc` bands are *less* plural than the `dti` ones. The honest reading: when the within-tier residual is essentially single-factor (income, here), the ε-band is non-empty and forward-valid but its members are the same model in different feature-subset clothing — there is *nothing to adjudicate*, no indeterminacy. Per my own pre-registered criterion (the claim requires plurality on a majority of grades), the claim is falsified on Burst A, and I am not moving that goalpost.

So the synthesis: **plurality is a property of the residual's structure (multi-factor → plural; single-factor → vacuous), not a guarantee of the policy-constrained Rashomon construction.** The construction reliably delivers a non-trivial, forward-valid, intrinsically-interpretable set at no predictive cost; whether that set has a *meaningful disagreement / indeterminacy dimension* depends on whether the miscalibration it's auditing is multi-factor.

## 4. What this establishes — for the superiority/observability argument

1. **On the episode that matters, the "plural set SHAP can't produce" claim stands.** 2015H2 is the burst the load-bearing positive rests on and the one the SHAP head-to-head was about. There, the policy-constrained Rashomon refinement set is non-trivial, plural (genuine member-disagreement, B1 down to ρ≈0.17), forward-predictively valid (6/7 grades), and free (constraint costs nothing). That is a set-level, intrinsically-interpretable, forward-validated governance artifact with a disagreement dimension — none of which a post-hoc surrogate-dependent SHAP attribution produces. The regulator-legibility / aggregate-model-risk-surveillance superiority axis is earned *here*.
2. **The plurality argument's scope is residual-structure-dependent — state this, don't over-claim.** On a single-factor miscalibration (the `annual_inc` burst), the Rashomon set is "true but not interesting": correct that there is structure, populated by equivalent models, but with no indeterminacy to surface. So the deliverable framing should be: "the policy-constrained Rashomon refinement set is a deployable, intrinsically-interpretable, forward-validated governance artifact at no predictive cost; *when the miscalibration is multi-factor* it additionally surfaces plurality and indeterminacy — a SHAP-incapable dimension — and the 2015H2 LC case is a worked instance; on single-factor miscalibration the plurality dimension is empty (still valid, just nothing to adjudicate)." Honest, and stronger than "always plural."
3. **The constraint is free.** Constrained-band best OOS AUC ≥ 0.9× (and usually ≈ or above) the unconstrained best on *every* grade, both bursts — and the kitchen-sink tree (named + 9 extension features, no monotonicity) does *not* materially beat the constrained band (often loses to it). So: the policy vocabulary + monotonicity directions cost essentially nothing in predictive power, and the 9 extra underwriting features add essentially nothing within these tiers. The "defensible *and* explainable, no tradeoff" point lands cleanly, and the "the policy should name more features" follow-on is *low priority* for these tiers — the named ones already carry the recoverable within-tier signal.
4. **The disagreement signal is real where it exists, and is the natural next probe.** Grade B1 (2015Q3): median pairwise member-Spearman 0.167. The borrowers on whom the band members most disagree are the "indeterminate" ones (`project_rashomon_methodology`'s within-Rashomon-disagreement → routing idea, instantiated on the pricing side with real magnitude). Characterizing that high-disagreement sub-population — and checking whether it has elevated realized-default variance — is the follow-on that turns "the set is plural" into "the set tells you *which cases* need human adjudication," which is the contemporaneous-observability product's actual point.

## 5. Caveats

- **Modest AUCs.** Best within-grade OOS AUCs ≈ 0.52–0.61 — a thin residual (the grade absorbed the cross-grade signal). The Rashomon set is a set of *thin* refinements; the plurality is plurality among models that each capture a little.
- **Pre-reg criterion choices.** ε = 0.02 (holdout AUC), the plurality bar (≥ 2 used-feature sets AND median Spearman < 0.9), the non-triviality bar (≥ 3 distinct members) were pinned before the run; the verdict is not threshold-fragile *on Burst D* (5/7 plural, several well below 0.9) but *is* near the line on Burst A (B3 at 0.908 is a hair's breadth from "plural"). A different ε or a Spearman bar of 0.92 would flip Burst A's verdict; the qualitative finding (Burst A's bands are *substantially* less plural than Burst D's) is robust to that.
- **De-dup interaction with the band size.** Many `(subset × depth × leaf)` combos fit the identical tree (depth-3 and depth-2 coincide if the tree stops early; leaf-25 and leaf-50 coincide if leaves are already large), so `distinct ≪ within-ε` on the larger bands (C1: 68 → 42; C5: 72 → 48). The non-triviality and plurality metrics use the de-duplicated set, which is the right thing — but the raw band size is not a meaningful plurality measure.
- **Single substrate / thin policy.** LC only; the thin-demo policy names 4 features (of which LC has all 4; the FM thin demo names `ltv`, available there — the FM variant would test the monotonicity constraint on a feature LC lacks). A real, richer policy would change the band — more named features → potentially more plurality.
