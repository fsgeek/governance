## 1. What was tested

The #6 policy-constrained refinement ε-bands (`scripts/within_tier_rashomon_test.py`) are built over the policy-**named** features `{fico_range_low, dti, annual_inc, emp_length}` only, so `d(x)` — the per-borrower disagreement among the distinct band members — is, by construction, a pure function of those four features; the disagreement-geometry test's P4 ("does `d` key on un-named structure?") could therefore never fire (ΔR² ≈ 0 trivially). This test repairs that: it rebuilds the Burst-D bands with the 9 **extension** underwriting features `{revol_util, open_acc, inq_last_6mths, delinq_2yrs, total_acc, mort_acc, loan_amnt, revol_bal, pub_rec}` **admitted into the candidate feature set** (the thin-demo policy's monotonicity directions still enforced on the named features — `{fico_range_low: −1, dti: +1}` in the default-prediction convention — the extension features unconstrained, because the documented policy says nothing about them), recomputes `d_ext`, and asks:

- **A** — do the band's distinct members actually *use* the extension features, or is the 4-feature policy vocabulary AUC-sufficient within these tiers at ε = 0.02 (in which case admitting them changes nothing)?
- **P4 (headline / "rescue")** — is `d_ext` still well-explained by the policy-**named** features alone (`R²_named ≥ 0.3`), or has the locus of disagreement moved to structure the policy vocabulary cannot name (`R²_named < 0.3` on a majority of headline-plural grades **AND** `ΔR²_ext = R²_all − R²_named ≥ 0.1` on a majority)? P4 firing = a piece of the (deflated, see `2026-05-12-disagreement-routing-result-note.md`) disagreement-routing claim rebuilt on a "the documented policy is blind to the contested feature" footing — which is ECOA/Reg-B disparate-impact adjacent.
- **P4-soft** — does the named explainer's grip on `d` drop by ≥ 15 R²-points relative to the #6 (named-only) band on a majority of headline grades (the "the disagreement *partly* moved off the policy vocabulary" reading)?
- **P5** — does admitting the extension features flip which grades are *plural* (was the policy constraint suppressing or inflating the indeterminacy)?

## 2. Setup

LC 36-month, Burst D (the load-bearing 2015H2 `dti` burst). Bands built on **V₁ = 2015Q3**; robustness arm on **V₂ = 2015Q4** (band frozen, `d_ext` recomputed on V₂'s grade-G loans). Flagged Cat-2-(pricing) grades in 2015Q3 read from `runs/pricing-lc-sweep-36mo-summary.json` (7: A1, A5, B1, C1, C2, C5, D4). Policy `policy/thin_demo_hmda.yaml`. Band construction identical to #6 except the candidate set = named ∪ extension (13 features in LC ⇒ 2¹³−1 = 8191 feature-subsets × {1,2,3} depths × {25,50,100} leaf-mins per grade): ε = 0.02, seed 0, 0.3 train/holdout split, de-dup by tree signature; distinct members refit on the full V₁ grade-G data, frozen; `d_ext(x)` = std over distinct members of predicted-default probability. Plurality (same #6 definition): ≥ 3 distinct members AND ≥ 2 distinct *used*-feature sets AND median pairwise ranking-Spearman among members < 0.9. The **headline-plural set** for the aggregate verdict = {plural under the extended band on V₁} ∩ {the #6 plural set: A5, B1, C1, C5, D4}; the extended-plural set, the became-plural and lost-plurality differences are all reported. Disagreement explainer (identical to the geometry test, so R² is comparable): regression CART, `max_depth ≤ 3`, `min_samples_leaf = 50`, 5-fold-CV R², predicting `d_ext` from (a) the named features, (b) named + extension; root-split feature, top-3 importances, partial-dependence shape of `d_ext` on the named-explainer root (`tails`/`threshold`/`monotone`/`flat`/`mixed`); univariate Spearman ρ(f, `d_ext`); threshold-proximity Spearman of `|dti − 43|` and `|fico_range_low − 620|`. Extension-usage diagnostic per grade: union over members of `used_feature_set ∩ extension`, fraction of members splitting on ≥ 1 extension feature, per-feature member counts. Comparison to #6 (the named-only band): `R²_named_ext` vs `R²_named_#6` (geometry result, V₁: A1 0.8611 [non-plural in #6], A5 0.4632, B1 0.5718, C1 0.6885, C2 0.5449 [non-plural], C5 0.4569, D4 0.5955); `ΔR²_band = R²_named_ext − R²_named_#6`.

**Verdict over the headline-plural grades {A5, B1, C1, C5} (n = 4; "majority" = strictly > 2, i.e. ≥ 3):**
- **A (band uses the extension features) — HIT.** 4/4 headline grades (and all 7 flagged grades) have ≥ 1 distinct member splitting on ≥ 1 extension feature; usually a large majority of members do (A5 92%, B1 75%, C1 100%, C5 100%; off-headline A1 99%, C2 99%, D4 38%). The policy's 4-feature vocabulary is **not AUC-sufficient** within these tiers at ε = 0.02 — admitting the 9 extension features substantially enlarges the admissible refinement set (band sizes balloon to 271–2906 distinct members vs the #6 named-only band's handful, with 15–158 distinct used-feature sets).
- **P4-strict (R²_named_ext < 0.3 on > maj AND ΔR²_ext ≥ 0.1 on > maj) — MISS.** ΔR²_ext ≥ 0.1 on **4/4** (the extension features always add ≥ 18 R²-points), but R²_named_ext < 0.3 on only **2/4** (C1 0.06, C5 0.13 — but A5 0.42, B1 0.42). 2 is not > 2, so the aggregate misses.
- **P4-soft (ΔR²_band ≤ −0.15 vs the #6 band on > maj) — HIT on V₁ (3/4: B1 −0.15, C1 −0.62, C5 −0.33; A5 only −0.05), FAILS TO REPLICATE on V₂ (2/4: B1 −0.14 just misses, C1 −0.58, C5 −0.29; A5 +0.02).** The named explainer's grip drops materially vs the #6 band on the grade-C tiers (robustly) and on B1 (fragile — right at the threshold). Report P4-soft as **fragile**: directionally there, not robust to the held-out quarter.
- **P5 (admitting the extension features flips plurality) — HIT, both directions.** **A1 and C2 became plural** (non-trivial-but-not-plural in #6; the extra used-feature sets the extension features create push median pairwise ρ below 0.9 and used-feature-set count ≥ 2). **D4 lost plurality** (median pairwise ρ rose to 0.926 > 0.9 — the extension-feature members are mostly low-AUC near-base-rate trees that rank D4's borrowers near-identically). Admitting the extension features doesn't uniformly expand or contract plurality — it reshuffles it.
- **The clean underlying pattern (the actual finding, sharper than the aggregate).** Across **all 7 flagged grades**, R²_named_ext (V₁) is: A1 0.66, A5 0.42, B1 0.42 — all ≥ 0.42, all `well_explained = True`; C1 0.06, C2 0.11, C5 0.13, D4 0.15 — all < 0.16, all `well_explained = False`. That's a **perfect split by letter grade**: with the band allowed the full underwriting feature set, the disagreement among policy-admissible refinements stays a legible function of the documented 4 features on the **prime tiers (A, B)** and **collapses off the policy vocabulary onto the extension features on the subprime-ish tiers (C, D)**. The headline "P4-strict misses" is an artifact of the headline set being 2 prime (A5, B1) + 2 subprime (C1, C5); the signal itself is clean and quality-stratified. Robust across V₁/V₂ (C1/C5/D4 stay < 0.17 on V₂; A5/B1 stay > 0.43).
- **Drivers/shapes (reproduce #6, V₁ = V₂):** A5→`dti`, B1→`annual_inc`, C1→`annual_inc`, C5→`dti`, D4→`annual_inc` — per-tier-idiosyncratic, matching #6's A5→dti / B1→annual_inc / C1→annual_inc. PD shape on the named-explainer root: A5/B1 `tails` (the prime tiers keep #6's "disagreement in the feature tails" geometry); C1/C5/D4 `mixed` (the subprime tiers, where the named root barely explains `d`, have no clean shape — consistent with "the structure isn't in the named features there"); A1 `monotone`.

## 4. How to apply / implications

- **The disagreement-routing deflation is partly reversed — and the reversal is *quality-stratified*.** On the subprime-ish tiers (C, D) the policy-admissible refinements fight over structure the documented 4-feature policy does not name (`inq_last_6mths`, `revol_util`, `revol_bal`, `mort_acc`, `delinq_2yrs`, `open_acc`, `total_acc`, `loan_amnt`, `pub_rec`) — exactly the "the documented policy is blind here → route to a human" case the disagreement-routing pre-reg was looking for, and the routable-population test ([[project_disagreement_routing_result]] §6, this note §6) can now be built directly on those tiers. On the prime tiers (A, B) the policy's 4 features remain an adequate vocabulary for the indeterminacy — the disagreement stays in `{fico, dti, annual_inc}`. So within-Rashomon disagreement, with the band allowed the full underwriting feature set, is a **tier-quality-stratified diagnostic of where the documented policy's vocabulary is inadequate to the indeterminacy**, not one portfolio-level phenomenon.
- **Fair-lending bite.** The tiers where the policy is blind to the contested feature are the **subprime** tiers — which is where ECOA/Reg-B disparate-impact concerns concentrate (subprime borrower pools skew toward protected classes via well-documented mechanisms). "The documented policy is silent about the feature the admissible refinements fight over" + "the silence is concentrated in the subprime tiers" = a specific, actionable fair-lending flag — this is the concrete content for the regulator-doc spine's Sonnet/Hamut'ay fair-lending add (ii), conditional no longer.
- **The #6 geometry's "`d` is purely named-legible" was a construction artifact — but only for the prime tiers.** Give the band the extension features and on A1/A5/B1 `d_ext` is still ~42–66% named-explained (eroded from the named-only band's ~46–86%, but not gone); on C1/C2/C5/D4 it drops to ~6–15%. So the geometry result's headline ("`d` is a legible low-dim function of the policy features") holds on the prime tiers and **inverts on the subprime tiers**.
- **Don't overclaim the rescue.** The headline aggregate verdict is "P4-strict miss, P4-soft fragile (V₁ only)." Report the *finding* — the quality-stratified split, which is robust (perfect across 7 grades, consistent V₁/V₂) — not a clean reversal of the deflation. The surviving aggregate-surveillance claim ([[project_disagreement_routing_result]]) is now refined: "a tier's policy-admissible refinements disagree at median rank-ρ X — and, on the subprime tiers, about features the documented policy doesn't name" is the model-risk + fair-lending fact a committee learns, tier-by-tier.
- **Codification-infrastructure data point ([[project_codification_infrastructure]]).** The thin demo's 4 features being predictively non-sufficient within these tiers (extension features competitive within a 2-AUC-point ε-band on every grade) is a concrete instance of "the thin demo does little work" — and a richer policy (#11, FNMA Selling Guide eligibility matrix) that *named* the extension-style features would change the candidate set and might close the subprime blindness; the test to run is the same one with a fuller `policy/*.yaml`.

## 5. Limits and methodological notes

- **Band-size blow-up at 13 candidate features.** The exact-tree-signature de-dup leaves bands of 271–2906 distinct members per grade, dominated by near-duplicate trees that fit on the same feature set and rank borrowers near-identically. Consequences: (i) the median-pairwise-Spearman plurality metric becomes less discriminating — B1's med-ρ is 0.563 here vs 0.167 in #6 (the bulk of similar pairs drags the median up; the *min* pairwise ρ and the *count of distinct used-feature sets* are the more meaningful plurality signals at this band size), and D4's med-ρ rose past 0.9 chiefly because the band filled with low-AUC near-base-rate trees; (ii) the O(members²) pairwise-Spearman and the `d` computation scale poorly (the run took ~45 min, dominated by the C2/C5/D4 bands). **A follow-up should de-dup by used-feature-set** (keep the highest-holdout-AUC representative per used-feature-set, ~15–160 members) — the more principled "distinct refinement" and what the disagreement-routing/geometry tests' `n_distinct_used_feature_sets` already counted. NB the geometry comparison (R²_named, driver, PD shape) is still apples-to-apples on `d`-*structure*; only the `d`-*magnitude* / plurality comparison is affected by the bloat.
- **The 9 extension features are LC-specific, not policy-derived.** "The band keys on un-named LC underwriting features on the subprime tiers" generalizes structurally to "richer policies need to name more, especially for subprime" — it is not a claim about any specific real lender's policy. A medium-fidelity codified policy (#11) is the way to make this concrete.
- **Depth-≤ 3 trees split on ≤ 7 features**, so the size-≥ 8 candidate subsets (most of the 8191) are largely de-dup-redundant — but they were in the registered design (§2 of the pre-reg) and the exact-signature de-dup handles exact matches; a size-cap (k ≤ 4) was considered as a runtime fix and not taken, to keep the run as registered.
- **No new `wedge/` code or unit tests.** The script reuses existing, tested helpers; `python -m pytest wedge/tests -q` is unchanged at 235 passed, 1 skipped.
