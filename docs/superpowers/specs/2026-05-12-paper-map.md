### Artifact 4 — The rung-4 / cross-jurisdiction paper  *(the "ignored and/or pisses people off" one)*

The documented-vs-operative gap has a *foundational floor*: where the framework itself (collateralized individual-title lending) presupposes a concept (alienable individual property) foreign to the community's operative land/economy relation (stewardship, reciprocity, communal tenure), the policy *cannot be checked* against the community's norms — the vocabulary to express "this land is inalienable" / "this economy redistributes rather than accumulates" doesn't exist in it. Cases: ayni (Andean); First Nations / Native American trust-land (the structural incompatibility — US BIA trust status, Canada Indian Act §89; conventional mortgages don't function on-reserve, hence HUD Section 184 / the First Nations Market Housing Fund as evidence the normal channel is broken; predatory fill via the "tribal lending" payday model); potlatch; communal land tenure generally. Codification's job at this rung: make the structural mismatch *nameable* — a constraint-type ontology that can represent inalienability, redistribution-norms, stewardship-relations as first-class. **The honest limits, prominent:** (a) legibility ≠ fix — naming the mismatch is a *precondition* for addressing it, not a solution; (b) *who codifies* matters — a settler-coded research lineage doing this codification *for* Indigenous communities reproduces the colonial move; the right framing is "here is the tool and the structural argument; the application must be community-led" — make space, don't speak for. Engages: legal-pluralism, decolonial/Indigenous economics, the financial-inclusion critique literature, Polanyi (embeddedness), property theory (Cobell v. Salazar etc.). Evidence: mostly conceptual + the documented harm record. Venue: critical-finance / law-and-society / decolonial-studies — explicitly *not* the ML or banking venues. May fission into a conceptual paper + an empirical (credit-desert / on-reserve-lending) companion.
