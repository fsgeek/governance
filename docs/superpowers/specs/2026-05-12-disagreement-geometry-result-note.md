# The geometry of within-Rashomon disagreement — RESULT

**Date:** 2026-05-12.
**Status:** Findings note (canonical for the run `runs/disagreement_geometry_test_results.json`, script `scripts/disagreement_geometry_test.py`, helpers in `wedge/routing.py` — the "Geometry of the disagreement" section, tests `wedge/tests/test_routing.py`). Companion to the pre-registration `docs/superpowers/specs/2026-05-12-disagreement-geometry-preregistration-note.md` — predictions and hit/miss criteria recorded before any code touched the data. Follow-on to the **disagreement-as-routing** result (`2026-05-12-disagreement-routing-result-note.md`).
**Headline:** **The post-hoc "residual-tracking" reading is confirmed and strengthened — `d(x)` is highly legible and low-dimensional in the policy features (CV R² 0.46–0.86 on every plural grade, recovered by a depth-≤3 tree on 1–2 features), and it concentrates in the *tails* of that feature (4/5 plural grades, 5/5 on the V₂ robustness arm) — so you can predict which borrowers the band will disagree on without ever running the band. But the dominant driver is *not* the same feature across the plural tiers: A5 → `dti`, B1 → `annual_inc`, C1 → `annual_inc`, C5 → `fico_range_low`, D4 → `dti` (identical on V₁ and V₂). The disagreement is per-tier-idiosyncratic — which *deepens* the routing deflation (it isn't even a coherent cross-tier signal) and nuances the "Burst D = the DTI burst" label (that's the *aggregate stratification* flag; the within-grade refinement disagreement disaggregates to different features per tier — and on the most-disagreeing grade, B1, the driver is *income*, not DTI). The pre-registered "rescue branch" (P4 — disagreement keys on un-named extension structure ⇒ a "the policy is blind here" routing trigger) **did not fire — and could not have**, because the #6 bands are built on the policy-named features only, so `d` is by construction a pure function of them (ΔR² ≈ 0.0000 from adding the 9 extension features). P4 as specified was vacuous; the proper rescue test needs a different band construction (extension features admitted) and is a followup. My cross-cutting pre-reg hedge — "`d` will be modest-but-partly-legible (R² 0.2–0.4) with a stubborn opaque fraction" — was wrong: within the named-feature span there is no opaque fraction; `d` is nearly determined by 1–2 policy features.**
**Authority:** Canonical for Burst D (LC 36-month, bands on 2015Q3, robustness on 2015Q4), 7 flagged Cat-2 grades / 5 plural. Not a generalization beyond LC / beyond the thin-demo policy vocabulary / beyond Burst D. The high R² is *partly* a near-mechanical consequence of the band construction (members are functions of the named features ⇒ their spread is too); the informative content is the *low-dimensionality* (a depth-3 tree suffices), the *per-tier feature heterogeneity*, and the *tails* shape.

## 1. What was run

For each of the 7 sub-grades flagged Cat-2-(pricing) in V₁ = 2015Q3 (A1, A5, B1, C1, C2, C5, D4), rebuild the policy-constrained refinement ε-band with the identical #6 construction (named features `{fico_range_low, dti, annual_inc, emp_length}`, monotonicity sign-flipped to default-prediction `{fico_range_low: −1, dti: +1}`, depths {1,2,3}, leaf-mins {25,50,100}, ε = 0.02, seed 0, de-dup by tree signature), refit its distinct members on the full V₁ grade (each on its own feature subset), and compute `d(x)` = std over members of predicted-default probability on every V₁ loan. The 5 plural grades re-derive the #6 way (≥ 2 distinct used-feature sets AND median pairwise ranking-Spearman among members < 0.9): **A5, B1, C1, C5, D4** (A1 ρ = 0.956, C2 ρ = 0.950 — non-trivial but not plural). Then per grade:

- **Disagreement explainer:** a depth-≤3 regression CART (`min_samples_leaf = 50`) predicting `d` from (a) the named features, (b) named + 9 extension underwriting features (`{revol_util, open_acc, inq_last_6mths, delinq_2yrs, total_acc, mort_acc, loan_amnt, revol_bal, pub_rec}`); scored by 5-fold-CV R²; root-split feature and top importances from a fit on all the data.
- **Univariate:** Spearman of each named feature with `d`; top-vs-bottom-decile mean `d`; tail/middle ratio.
- **PD profile:** mean `d` across deciles of the named explainer's root feature; coarse shape label — `tails` (max in an end bucket, larger-tail ≥ 1.5× the middle-8 mean), `threshold` (interior bump ≥ 1.3× the middle), `monotone` (|Spearman(decile index, decile mean)| ≥ 0.8), `flat`, `mixed`.
- **Threshold proximity:** Spearman of `|dti − 43|` and `|fico_range_low − 620|` with `d`.

`d` recomputed on V₂ = 2015Q4's grade loans (members frozen, same band) for a robustness arm. Verdict (pre-reg §3) aggregates over the 5 plural grades on V₁ (primary): P1 well-explained (CV R² ≥ 0.3 OR |Spearman(root, d)| ≥ 0.3) on ≥ 3/5; P2 same single root feature on ≥ 3/5 (P2-dti: that feature is `dti`); P3 `tails` on ≥ 3/5 (P3-threshold: `threshold` on ≥ 3/5); P4 ΔR² ≥ 0.1 on ≥ 3/5 AND named R² < 0.3 on ≥ 3/5.

**Note on the high R² vs the low univariate |Spearman|.** `d` is *well-explained by a tree* (R² 0.46–0.86) but only *weakly rank-correlated* with its own root feature (|Spearman| 0.05–0.31). No contradiction: `d` is a *non-monotone* (mostly U-shaped) function of the root feature, so a tree captures it but a rank correlation barely does. "Well-explained" fires on the R² limb, not the ρ limb, on every grade — and the R² limb is the right one to trust here.

## 3. Results — V₂ robustness arm (members frozen, `d` recomputed on 2015Q4)

Highly consistent with V₁: **same root feature per grade** (A5→`dti`, B1→`annual_inc`, C1→`annual_inc`, C5→`fico_range_low`, D4→`dti`); P1 hit (CV R² 0.43–0.80); P3 *stronger* (5/5 `tails` — C1 reads `tails` on V₂ where V₁ read `mixed`); P4 miss (ΔR² ≈ 0.0000). The per-tier feature pattern is stable across the two quarters — not a fluke of one vintage.

## 5. What this means

- **The mechanism reading is now a tested claim, and it holds (strongly).** `d(x)` ≈ "where the within-tier residual feature is active" — and the feature is one of `{dti, annual_inc, fico_range_low}` per tier, with `d` peaking in that feature's *tails*. You can predict the band's disagreement from a depth-3 tree on 1–2 policy features; it's not a black-box quantity. Concretely, on B1 (the most-disagreeing tier): the band disagrees most about the *lowest-income* borrowers (and somewhat the highest), least about the middle — a legible, statable population.
- **The routing deflation is deepened, not lifted.** Yesterday: disagreement doesn't localize to the hard cases (and is mildly anti-correlated with unreliability). Today: it isn't even keyed to a *consistent* feature across the plural tiers — A5 and D4 disagree about debt-to-income, B1 and C1 about income, C5 about FICO. So "the band's disagreement" is not one phenomenon; it's a per-tier artifact of which residual feature each tier's refinements happen to fight over. That further undercuts any per-case routing claim, and it also means the *aggregate-surveillance* claim ([[project_disagreement_routing_result]]) has to be stated tier-by-tier ("B1's refinements disagree about income"), not as a portfolio-level "the refinements disagree."
- **The "Burst D = the DTI burst" framing is a stratification-test statement, not a refinement statement.** The grade-level Cat-2 flag was DTI-keyed for the 2015 vintages *in aggregate* ([[project_pricing_space_cat2]]); the within-grade refinement-band disagreement disaggregates to `annual_inc` on 2 of the 5 plural grades and `fico` on 1. Keep these layers distinct in the paper: the aggregate stratification flag and the per-tier refinement-disagreement geometry are different objects.
- **The rescue branch is alive but needs a different experiment.** "Does the band disagree about structure the documented policy can't name?" cannot be answered with #6's bands (named-features-only). It needs a band construction that *admits* the extension features (and then asks whether the named-feature explainer's R² on `d` drops, with the extension features picking up the slack). If *that* fires, the "policy is blind here → route to a human" trigger is back on firmer footing than the per-case-uncertainty version. This is the single clean followup; see §6.
- **For the regulator-facing account:** the picture to carry is "within-Rashomon disagreement is a legible, low-dimensional, *tier-specific* function of one policy feature — it tells you *what* the admissible refinements of a given tier are fighting over (income, DTI, FICO), which is a real auditable model-risk fact about that tier — but it does not give you a per-case route-to-human trigger, and it is not a single portfolio-level phenomenon." That's honest, it's specific, and it's a stronger statement than either "disagreement routes" (false) or "disagreement is noise" (also false — it's structured, just not routable).
