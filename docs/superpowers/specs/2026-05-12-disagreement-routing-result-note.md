# Within-tier disagreement as a routing signal — RESULT

**Date:** 2026-05-12.
**Status:** Findings note (canonical for the run `runs/disagreement_routing_test_results.json`, script `scripts/disagreement_routing_test.py`, module `wedge/routing.py`, tests `wedge/tests/test_routing.py`). Companion to the pre-registration `docs/superpowers/specs/2026-05-12-disagreement-routing-preregistration-note.md` — predictions and hit/miss criteria recorded before any code touched the held-out quarter. Follow-on to **#6** (`2026-05-12-policy-constrained-rashomon-refinement-result-note.md`).
**Headline:** **Deflating result — the pre-registered ≈30%-probability branch is what occurred. On the 5 plural grades of Burst D, per-borrower disagreement among policy-admissible refinement members does *not* function as a per-case routing signal: metrics 1/2/3 point the routing-relevant way on only 2 / 1 / 2 of the 5 grades respectively (a majority *against* on all three), and metric 4's operational lift is operationally negligible everywhere (|ΔECE| ≤ 0.0095). If anything the sign is mildly *reversed* — the high-disagreement tercile tends to be slightly *more* predictable and *better*-calibrated, not less. Reading: disagreement among the refinements localizes to where the within-tier residual feature (DTI) is doing work — i.e. where there *is* signal — not to "the hard cases"; high-disagreement borrowers are also mildly feature-space-extrapolated (Mahalanobis high/low ratio 1.1–1.65) but that extrapolation doesn't degrade the consensus there.** So the plurality-superiority argument must rest on **aggregate model-risk surveillance** (the bank sees that policy-admissible refinements of a tier disagree — itself an auditable, SHAP-incapable fact that the tier's pricing is underdetermined within the policy vocabulary), **not** on per-case auto-decide-vs-route. The README's adversarial-review device still works as a *presentation* layer for a case a human is already reviewing; it does not *select* those cases.
**Authority:** Canonical for Burst D (LC 36-month, 2015Q3 → 2015Q4), the 7 flagged Cat-2 grades / 5 plural. Within-grade AUCs are thin (0.50–0.64); ECE values are small (0.007–0.046). Not a generalization beyond LC / beyond the thin-demo policy vocabulary / beyond Burst D (Burst A — non-plural in #6 — is a followup, §6, not run here).
**Depends on:** `wedge/routing.py`, `wedge/refinement_set.py` (the #6 band construction), `wedge/predictive.py` (`shuffle_null_auc` semantics, reused), `scripts/disagreement_routing_test.py`, `runs/pricing-lc-sweep-36mo-summary.json` (flagged-grade lists), `policy/thin_demo_hmda.yaml` + `policy/encoder.py` (monotonicity directions), `data/accepted_2007_to_2018Q4.csv`. ## 1. What was run

Burst D of #6 (the load-bearing 2015H2 `dti` burst): build on V₁ = 2015Q3, route-evaluate on V₂ = 2015Q4. For each of the 7 sub-grades flagged Cat-2-(pricing) in V₁ (A1, A5, B1, C1, C2, C5, D4 — from `runs/pricing-lc-sweep-36mo-summary.json`), rebuild the policy-constrained refinement ε-band (`wedge.refinement_set.build_refinement_band`, identical params to #6: named features `{fico_range_low, dti, annual_inc, emp_length}`, monotonicity sign-flipped to default-prediction `{fico_range_low: −1, dti: +1}`, depths {1,2,3}, leaf-mins {25,50,100}, ε = 0.02, seed 0, de-dup by tree signature), refit its distinct members on the full V₁ grade (each on its own feature subset, frozen), and re-derive plurality the #6 way (≥ 2 distinct used-feature sets AND median pairwise ranking-Spearman among members < 0.9). The plural set re-derives to exactly **{A5, B1, C1, C5, D4}** (A1 ρ = 0.956, C2 ρ = 0.950 — non-trivial but not plural), matching #6.

On V₂'s grade-G loans: `d(x)` = std (ddof 0) over band members of their predicted-default probability on x; `p̄(x)` = mean over members; terciles of `d(x)` → low / middle / high disagreement (rank-based, equal-sized buckets). Then the four pre-registered metrics + a diagnostic:

1. **Tercile predictability.** Per disagreement-tercile, the mean over band members of that member's within-tercile AUC for realized default, vs a grade-size-aware label-shuffle null (95th pct, 500 perms, per tercile). Routing-relevant if low-tercile mean AUC > high-tercile mean AUC, with low above its null and high weaker (≤ its null, or simply below low).
2. **Disagreement vs consensus calibration gap.** Bin V₂'s grade-G loans into deciles of `d(x)`; per decile `|mean realized default − mean p̄|`; Spearman of (decile index, gap). Routing-relevant if positive.
3. **Disagreement vs consensus Brier.** Per `d`-tercile, consensus Brier `mean((p̄ − y)²)`; report high − low. Routing-relevant if positive.
4. **Operational lift.** ECE (size-weighted, deciles of `p̄`) on *all* of V₂'s grade-G loans vs on the *low + middle* `d`-terciles only (high-`d` tercile routed to manual review, removed from the auto-refined population). `lift = ECE_all − ECE_auto_kept`; routing-relevant if non-negative.

*Diagnostic (not a pre-registered hit criterion, but the pre-reg said it would be reported):* mean Mahalanobis distance of each `d`-tercile's V₂ rows under V₁'s tier mean/covariance (pinv) — is the high-`d` tercile feature-space-extrapolated?

Aggregate verdict over the 5 plural grades (pre-reg §3): "disagreement is a valid routing signal" holds iff metrics 1–3 point routing-relevant on a majority (≥ 3/5) of the plural grades, with metric 4 non-negative on a majority. **Falsified** iff a majority show metrics 1–3 *not* pointing the routing way. A robustness arm reports the same metrics in-sample on V₁ (members already fit on full V₁ → in-sample scores).

**Why the prediction was wrong (the substantive finding).** The pre-reg assumed a uniformity: *disagreement = underdetermination = the consensus is unreliable there*. The data say disagreement ≈ *where the within-tier residual feature (DTI, here) is doing work* — depth-1/2/3 trees over different feature subsets carve an active residual gradient differently (→ spread of predicted probabilities), while in the tier-interior region where the grade has already absorbed the risk every member returns ≈ the base rate (→ no spread). So `d(x)` is closer to a "is there residual structure near x" indicator than an "are the models confused about x" indicator — and where there *is* residual structure, the refinements collectively (the consensus mean) predict somewhat *better*, not worse. The high-`d` borrowers being mildly feature-extrapolated is consistent with this (the active within-tier decision regions sit toward the feature-space edges of a thin homogeneous tier). This is another instance of the project's recurring [[project_pre_registration_pattern]] — the pre-registered prediction baked in a uniformity that the heterogeneity (disagreement tracks signal, not confusion) overturned; the heterogeneity is the result.

## 5. What this means for the Olorin / regulator argument

- **Drop the per-case routing claim for the policy-constrained refinement band — at least as instantiated here.** "The set is plural" does *not* upgrade to "the set tells you which borrowers need a human." Member disagreement does not select the hard cases; on this burst it mildly anti-selects them.
- **Keep the plurality-superiority claim in its *aggregate / surveillance* form.** That a tier's policy-admissible refinements disagree at median rank-Spearman 0.17 (B1) is a real, auditable, model-internal fact: *the tier's pricing is underdetermined within the documented policy vocabulary — the policy doesn't pin down who is higher-risk inside this tier*. A model-risk committee learns something true and actionable at the *tier* level (this tier's price warrants scrutiny / the policy vocabulary is too thin for this tier), and SHAP-on-a-surrogate structurally cannot produce a "set of admissible models" to disagree in the first place. That is the surviving — weaker but genuine — version of the superiority axis ([[project_strategic_argument]], [[project_shap_pricing_result]] for the parallel "scope the claim, don't over-claim" move).
- **The README's adversarial-review device survives as a *presentation* layer, not a triage layer.** For a case a human is *already* reviewing (selected by some other trigger — a complaint, a price-appeal, an audit sample), surfacing the two most-disagreeing band members as "advocates" with their differing feature emphases is still a clean way to frame the residual decision. But disagreement does not *choose* the review queue.
- **Carry into the regulator-facing document:** the contemporaneous monitor's auto-decide-vs-route core, if it has one, has to come from somewhere other than refinement-member disagreement — e.g. consensus-prediction *uncertainty* against the grade-implied rate, or the per-tier significance flag from the stratification test (which *is* tier-level and *did* validate forward) — not from intra-Rashomon spread. The disagreement-routing idea, as a per-case mechanism, did not survive contact with the held-out quarter; report it as a tested-and-rejected design alternative (which is more useful to a regulator than an untested aspiration).
